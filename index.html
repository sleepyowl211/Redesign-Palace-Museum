The Palace Museum: Responsive Redesign

Located in Beijing, China, the Palace Museum is one of my favorite spots in my hometown. I found out that the English version of its official website is very difficult to navigate, so I decided to redesign it for better responsiveness and accessibility.

Original webpage: The Palace Museum

Problem Identification

  1. Consistency and standards: some of the elements are overlapped when the website is opened from devices of different sizes.
  2. Flexibility: the images on the website would not adjust according to the screen size.
  3. Aesthetic and minimalist design: the fonts are not visible on some of the image backgrounds.
  4. Help and documentation: the navigation menu design makes it difficult to locate information.
  5. The efficiency of use: the website loads very slowly and has a very long response time after I click on the interactive elements.

Accessibility

The website is not designed in an accessible way. In total, there are 82 errors and 52 alerts. The main problem is the lack of alternative text (55 linked images without alternative text), low visibility (13 contrast error and 5 very small texts), and unnecessary elements (15 empty links, 6 empty buttons, and 44 redundant links). I believe that more readable text and image design, as well as the implementation of alternative texts, would help improve the accessibility of the website.
